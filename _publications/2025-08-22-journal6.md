Abstract: With the decarbonization target to combat climate change, the trend towards decentralization of energy markets, and the natural coupling of electricity and carbon trading, it is necessary to build a Peer-to-Peer (P2P) joint energy and carbon (E&C) trading mechanism. One of the main challenges is that the decentralized negotiation process is easy to cause private information leakage, e.g., generation cost curve, consumption behavior, and carbon emission intensity. In this paper, we propose a novel privacy-preserving architecture for P2P joint E&C trading. A decentralized optimization (DO)-based negotiation mechanism is first developed for simultaneously clearing energy and carbon emission permit. Then, we investigate how to protect the private information under this negotiation mechanism in three aspects. First, we design a secure amounts and prices update mechanism combining homomorphic encryption (HE) and DO; Second, we realize secure verification of transaction results without revealing real value using zero knowledge proof (ZKP); Third, a main-side double-blockchain system is established to store energy and carbon transaction results separately, and realize cross-chain secure verification. Simulation results show the convergence performance, social welfare, computational efficiency and scalability with and without privacy protection.
